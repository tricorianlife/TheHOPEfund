The people behind the hope.

Our board brings together leading surgeons, clinicians and professionals united by one goal: better outcomes for people facing upper GI and HPB cancers.

Our founding board members are Michael Hii, Adrian Fox and James Ostroburski. Since that time, fellow gastrointestinal surgeons and other medical professionals, including nurses, allied health professionals and researchers, along with senior Kooyong Group partners, have joined the board to support the work of the Fund.

While very busy with clinical practice, all of our board members maintain a strong interest in research and play active roles in surgical training and teaching through universities in Melbourne.

Deep specialist expertise

Our board includes highly trained medical experts in upper gastrointestinal and hepato-pancreatic biliary surgery, with specialisations in diseases of the oesophagus, stomach and adrenal glands, oesophageal and stomach cancer, liver transplantation, endoscopic surgery, reflux disease, hiatal hernia, and bariatric, liver and pancreatic surgery.

Board of directors

Meet the board.

Michael Hii
Chairperson

A founding board member and upper gastrointestinal surgeon at St Vincent’s Hospital Melbourne, Michael leads the unit’s research team. He sets the Fund’s research direction and connects donors directly to the clinical work their gifts support.

Adrian Fox
Vice Chairperson

A founding board member and hepatobiliary surgeon, Adrian specialises in liver, pancreas and bile duct surgery. As Vice Chairperson he steers the Fund’s strategy and keeps its research priorities grounded in what surgeons see in theatre.

Peter Wilson
Treasurer

As Treasurer, Peter Wilson safeguards the Fund’s finances, making sure every donation reaches the research and education programs it was given to.

Henry Badgery
Secretary

A surgical registrar and PhD candidate at St Vincent’s Hospital Melbourne, Henry researches how technology can improve upper gastrointestinal surgery. As Secretary he keeps the board organised and brings a trainee’s perspective to its decisions.

Lynn Chong
Board member

Dr Lynn Chong holds a PhD in epithelial stem cell biology and coordinates research projects and clinical trials for the Upper Gastrointestinal and Hepatobiliary unit at St Vincent’s. She helps the Fund turn donor support into published research and training for the next generation of surgeons.

Matthew Read
Board member

Professor Matthew Read is a board member of The HOPE Fund and a long standing advocate for upper gastrointestinal cancer research. He lends the Fund scientific credibility and a surgeon’s view on which projects will genuinely change patient outcomes.

Nicole Winter
Board member

An upper gastrointestinal and bariatric surgeon at the Royal Melbourne Hospital and St Vincent’s, Nicole sits on the ANZGOSA board. She brings deep clinical expertise in oesophageal and stomach surgery and a commitment to surgeon led research.

Anne Read
Board member

Anne brings deep operational and organisational experience from the health sector to the board. She keeps the Fund’s day to day running smoothly, letting clinicians focus on research and patient care.

Daniel Pote
Board member

A commercial director with a track record of delivering exceptional financial results, Dan Pote brings entrepreneurial leadership to the board. He is passionate about developing others and helps the Fund grow its reach and impact.

Melissa Hii
Board member

Melissa Hii supports the Fund’s mission to improve outcomes for patients with upper gastrointestinal disease, lending her time and energy to its community and fundraising efforts.

Roz Walmsley
Board member

A researcher in the Department of Hepatobiliary and Upper Gastrointestinal Surgery at St Vincent’s, Dr Roz Walmsley studies how bariatric surgery changes the body’s response to food. Her work helps the Fund build the evidence base for better surgical care.

Dinesh Samarasinghe
Board member

Dinesh Samarasinghe is the CEO and Founder of Tricorian Life, with more than 15 years of leadership in healthcare transformation, diagnostics and digital health. He brings commercial and strategy expertise that helps the Fund scale its research and education programs.

Christopher Farrell
Chief Executive Officer

As Chief Executive Officer, Christopher Farrell leads the Fund’s operations and growth, drawing on a career built in healthcare. He works with the board and clinical team to turn donor generosity into research, education and better patient journeys.
