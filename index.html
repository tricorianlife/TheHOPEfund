Our mission

More than a charity.
A movement.

In 2018, a group of Melbourne clinicians with a shared passion came together to change what was possible for their patients.

What began as an initiative by hepatic, pancreatic, biliary and upper gastrointestinal surgeons and nurses quickly gained momentum. Today, The HOPE Fund pursues the best possible outcomes for all upper GI and HPB cancers through life-changing research, education and patient care.

Despite having some of the lowest survival rates of all major cancers, these diseases remain underfunded and often overlooked. Each day, we work to give patients more than just hope. We deliver relentless innovation in research, surgery and clinical practice.

Why we exist

Founded by surgeons who refused to accept the odds.

Every day, our surgeons operate on some of the hardest cancers there are. And every day, they watched too many patients run out of options. In 2018, a group of Melbourne’s hepatic, pancreatic, biliary and upper GI surgeons and nurses decided that wasn’t good enough, and came together to build something their patients had never had before.

They built The HOPE Fund: a charity where the people doing the surgery also drive the research, train the next generation, and sit beside patients through recovery. No distance between the operating theatre and the laboratory. No gap between the donor and the difference their gift makes.

“We came together for one reason: so that no patient facing these cancers ever faces them alone.”

The story in our logo

A tree of life, drawn from within.

Most people see a tree. Our surgeons see something else entirely.

Our logo is the biliary tree, the very real anatomical name for the branching network of bile ducts that carries bile from the liver and gallbladder. It is where so many of the cancers we fight begin. Designed by one of our founding surgeons, it transforms the anatomy we treat into a symbol of the life we protect.

  • 1
    The trunkThe common bile duct, the main channel, strong and central.
  • 2
    The branchesThe hepatic ducts, reaching into the liver like limbs into sky.
  • 3
    The leavesThe fine ductules, and every patient whose life continues to grow.
  • 4
    The rootsOur community of donors, surgeons and researchers, holding everything up.

“We took the anatomy that breaks our patients’ lives and turned it into the thing that gives them back.”

Every dollar accounted for

Your gift, tracked to the last dollar.

Accountability was part of the founders’ vision from day one: more of every donation reaching the research and patient-care teams who need it, with donors able to see exactly where their gift goes.

01

You give

Every donation is receipted and tax-deductible over $2, through our DGR status with the ATO.

02

We allocate

Funds are assigned to a named research, education or patient-care project, never a general pool.

03

Teams deliver

Surgeons, researchers and nurses run the work, with progress reported against each project.

04

You see it

Outcomes are published openly, in papers, trials and patient milestones, so impact is visible, not assumed.

Research & programs

Research that reaches patients.

We fund world-class research and patient-care initiatives across Melbourne’s leading hospitals, in partnership with St Vincent’s Hospital and the surgical training programs of RACS.

Clinical trial

HIIT Cancer Project

A randomised controlled trial of high-intensity interval training in the immediate post-operative period for patients undergoing major foregut cancer surgery, because recovery after cancer surgery is unique to every person.

Learn more →
Technology

AI in Surgery

Applying artificial intelligence and machine learning to improve surgical decision-making, object recognition and outcomes, pushing the boundaries of what’s possible in the operating theatre.

Learn more →
Registry

MAPS Pancreatic Cyst Registry

A national database following patients with IPMN, precancerous pancreatic lesions, to identify risk factors, enable early detection and reduce unnecessary surveillance and anxiety.

Learn more →
Research

Body Composition Studies

Prospective studies characterising muscle and fat loss in gastric, oesophageal and bariatric patients across treatment, to improve delivery of therapy and survival.

Learn more →
First in Australia

Synbiotics & Surgical Infection

Australia’s first randomised controlled trial investigating pre-operative prebiotic and probiotic therapy to reduce infectious complications after major upper abdominal surgery.

Learn more →
Clinical trial

Akynzeo® PONV Trial

A randomised controlled trial comparing Akynzeo® (netupitant/palonosetron) with standard treatment to prevent post-operative nausea and vomiting after bariatric surgery, assessing efficacy, safety and cost-effectiveness for a smoother recovery.

Learn more →
Education

Surgical Fellowships & Training

18-month fellowships raising global standards in surgical practice, from robotic surgery in Utrecht to training international surgeons like Dr Keith Dindi through OESO collaboration.

Questions, answered

Everything donors ask us.

Is my donation tax-deductible?

Yes. The HOPE Fund holds Deductible Gift Recipient (DGR) status with the Australian Tax Office, so donations of AUD $2.00 or more are tax-deductible. You will receive a receipt for every gift.

How is my donation used?

Donations fund research, education and patient-care programs for upper GI and HPB cancers. Every dollar is tracked and allocated to a specific project, so you can see the impact of your gift. Our annual reporting is published through the ACNC.

What cancers does The HOPE Fund support?

Upper GI and hepatobiliary (HPB) cancers: pancreatic, liver, stomach, biliary tract and oesophageal cancers. These are among the most underfunded cancers, with some of the lowest survival rates, which is exactly why we focus here.

Who founded The HOPE Fund?

A group of Melbourne’s hepatic, pancreatic, biliary and upper GI surgeons and nurses, who came together in 2018 to transform outcomes for their patients through research, education and patient care.

How else can I support the fund?

Become a Champion of HOPE through ongoing giving, sponsor an event like A Night of HOPE, partner with us as an organisation, or share a story of hope. Email contact@thehopefund.org.au and we will find the right fit.
